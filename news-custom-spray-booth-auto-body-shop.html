Custom Automotive Spray Booth

Why auto body shops choose a custom spray booth

Every repair shop has a different building, vehicle mix, energy supply, and workflow. A custom spray booth helps the equipment match the daily working site.

Custom size makes daily work easier

A standard automotive spray booth can be suitable for many passenger car repair shops, but it is not the best answer for every site. If a shop handles SUVs, vans, or vehicles with roof accessories, a larger inner clearance may improve daily operation. If the workshop has limited space, a compact repair shop paint booth may be a better fit.

Custom dimensions help the booth match the available building space while still allowing enough room for technicians to move around the vehicle safely and efficiently.

Airflow and exhaust should match the building

Some buildings allow a simple exhaust route. Others need a more careful duct plan because of nearby walls, roof structure, or local site restrictions. Custom airflow planning helps the booth work with the building instead of forcing the building to adapt to a fixed design.

For repair shops, this matters because the booth should support stable finish quality while also being practical to install and maintain. Filter replacement, floor access, lighting service, and exhaust duct inspection should be considered before production.

Heating and voltage are not the same in every country

Overseas buyers often have different voltage, phase, frequency, and heating preferences. A custom auto body shop spray booth can be prepared according to the local power supply and preferred heating method. This helps the buyer avoid extra changes after the booth arrives.

Before quotation, the buyer should confirm voltage, available power capacity, local fuel preference, and whether electric, gas, diesel, or infrared heating is more suitable for the project.

A custom booth needs clear communication

Custom configuration is useful only when the supplier understands the site details. Repair shops should share workshop drawings, photos, vehicle size, daily refinishing needs, and any local installation restrictions. Gubot uses this information to discuss booth size, airflow, heating, lighting, filtration, control system, and delivery plan.
